# REGULUS: Privacy Policy Regulatory Review

## APPLICABLE REGULATIONS

- **GDPR Art. 13**: Information to be provided when personal data are collected from the data subject
- **GDPR Art. 14**: Information to be provided when personal data have not been obtained from the data subject
- **GDPR Art. 15**: Right of access by the data subject
- **GDPR Art. 16**: Right to rectification
- **GDPR Art. 17**: Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten")
- **GDPR Art. 18**: Right to restriction of processing
- **GDPR Art. 20**: Right to data portability
- **GDPR Art. 21**: Right to object
- **GDPR Art. 6**: Lawfulness of processing (legal basis)
- **GDPR Art. 30**: Records of processing activities
- **GDPR Art. 77**: Right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority

## RISK FLAGS

### ⚠️ MINOR ISSUES (Non-blocking)

1. **Response Time Precision**
   - **Current**: "We will respond within 30 days"
   - **GDPR Requirement**: Art. 12(3) requires response "without undue delay and in any event within one month"
   - **Recommendation**: Change to "within one month (30 days)" for strict legal accuracy

2. **Missing Article Citations for Rights**
   - **Current**: Rights listed without article numbers
   - **Recommendation**: Add article citations for clarity:
     - Access — Art. 15
     - Rectification — Art. 16
     - Erasure — Art. 17
     - Portability — Art. 20
     - Restriction — Art. 18
     - Object — Art. 21

3. **Privacy Policy Legal Basis**
   - **Current**: No explicit mention that this privacy policy fulfills Art. 13/14 obligations
   - **Recommendation**: Add introductory note: "This privacy policy fulfills our obligations under GDPR Art. 13 (information to be provided when personal data are collected from the data subject)"

4. **Art. 30 Retention Period**
   - **Current**: "retained for the duration of your account — these records support your GDPR Art. 30 obligations"
   - **Issue**: Art. 30 requires records to be kept, but doesn't specify retention period. The statement is correct but could clarify that Art. 30 requires records to be maintained and made available to supervisory authorities.
   - **Recommendation**: No change required, but consider: "retained for the duration of your account — Art. 30 requires controllers to maintain records of processing activities"
